"The medical way of dealing with such problems -- with the inevitable nuances of an individual patient case -- is to leave them to the expert's individual judgment. You give the specialist autonomy."

Atul Gawande, The Checklist Manifesto, p. 62

"In the absence of a true Master Builder -- a supreme, all-knowing expert with command of all existing knowledge -- autonomy is a disaster. It produces only a cacophony of incompatible decisions and overlooked errors."

Atul Gawande, The Checklist Manifesto, p. 63

"[Discipline] is a concept almost entirely outside the lexicon of most professions, including my own. In medicine, we hold up "autonomy" as a professional lodestar, a principle that stands in direct opposition to discipline. But in [the current world] ... individual autonomy hardly seems the ideal we should aim for. It has the ring more of protectionism than of excellence."

Atul Gawande, The Checklist Manifesto, p. 182
